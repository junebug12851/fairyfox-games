# 2026-07-12

## GROW — Skyline onto varied structure (the wind). v0.21.1.

**The gap.** Skyline was one of three games still off the varied-structure pattern (with Loft and
Poise). Its slab came from a single flat rule in `spawnCurrent`: a random edge-safe start, a random
heading, the score's slide speed. The *only* thing that ever changed across a run — or between runs
— was how fast the slab slid. Every tower rose the same way.

**The change.** A run is now a seeded **sequence of named wind patterns**. Copied Polarity's shape
into Skyline's own core (`FORMATIONS` / `pickFormation` / `loadFormation`, self-contained — no
shared runtime); `spawnCurrent` pulls one `{fx, dir, speedMul}` spec at a time and refills from a
fresh, stage-eligible formation when the queue is spent.

| Pattern | minStage | Notable | What it does |
|---|---|---|---|
| Steady | 0 | — | the calm on-ramp (the old generator, kept) |
| Crosswind | 0 | — | slabs enter hard against alternating edges; long, readable sweeps |
| Plumb Line | 1 | ✔ | wind drops — slow (0.75×), near-centre slabs: the **flush-streak window** |
| Gust | 1 | ✔ | a short run of fast slabs (1.22–1.40×) thrown in from an edge |
| Shear | 2 | ✔ | crawling ↔ racing slabs (0.8× / 1.42×) — rhythm is useless, read each one |
| The Squall | 3 | ✔ | Spire-only crescendo: near-max-speed slabs (1.45–1.55×), alternating edges |

`minStage` gates each → climbing the stages **opens the pool** (progression drives the variation);
`weight(stage)` leans on the wild wind late. Calm share falls **>75% (Foundation) → <40% (Spire)**,
pinned by a test.

**The design call worth recording: the wind is a *multiplier on the honest ramp*, not a new axis.**
`slabSpeed()` = `speedOf()` × the formation's `speedMul`, clamped to `[SPEED_MUL_MIN=0.7,
SPEED_MUL_MAX=1.55]` and hard-capped at `SPEED_HARD_MAX=12`. So a formation can *shape* the
difficulty the score already earned, but can never spike past it — the varied-structure standard's
"honest difficulty" guardrail, made structural rather than a promise. A slab with no `speedMul`
(e.g. one a test builds by hand) rides the plain ramp, so the legacy shape still works.

**Plumb Line is the interesting one.** It's the *only* formation that makes the game easier — and
that's the point: it's the greed beat. Slow, centred slabs are the best flush-streak window in the
game, and Skyline's escalating streak bonus (2nd flush +1, 3rd +2 …) means noticing it and
committing is worth real points. It's notable, so it announces itself. That's a decision the player
gets to make, which is more interesting than a difficulty spike.

**Legibility.** The cue (`◇ PLUMB LINE`) names notable patterns only; calm ones pass silently. And
the wind reads on the canvas *before* it's named: a slab with `speedMul > 1.1` drags a short motion
streak and burns brighter (`prefers-reduced-motion` honoured, streak suppressed). Start-panel copy
names the pattern set so a new player knows runs vary.
