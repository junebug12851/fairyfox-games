## PLANT тАФ new game: Brim (a new verb: pour/fill). v0.22.0.

**The brief.** Ship one brand-new, mechanically *distinct* game. The verbs already spent across the
12 existing games: steer ┬╖ time-a-catch ┬╖ thrust/physics ┬╖ flip-match ┬╖ aim-bounce ┬╖ precision-stack
┬╖ keep-aloft/rhythm ┬╖ balance ┬╖ mirror-coordination ┬╖ judge-power/charge ┬╖ sort/route ┬╖
swing/grapple. Nothing in the collection asks you to **meter a quantity** тАФ to watch a value rise
and stop it inside a band. So: **pour/fill**.

**The design, and the one idea it's built on.** A vessel has a **fill line** you must reach and a
**rim** you must not cross. Hold to pour, let go to stop тАФ *one pour per vessel*, so the release is
the only decision there is. The hook is that **the stream has to fall**: the pour is a delay line
(`CONFIG.LAG = 8` ticks тЙИ 133 ms), so letting go does not stop the level rising, it stops the
*source* тАФ and the column already in the air (`carry()`) lands regardless. You therefore cannot stop
where you want. You must stop **early, by exactly the amount still falling**, and then watch it come
down.

That single physical fact generates the whole game without anything being bolted on:

- stop too early тЖТ **short** of the line тЖТ a life;
- stop too late тЖТ the carry pushes it over the rim тЖТ **spill** тЖТ a life;
- stop so it settles in the **gold band** under the rim тЖТ a **brim**: the multiplier climbs
  (├Ч2тАж├Ч9), while a timid-but-safe land still scores and *breaks* the combo back to ├Ч1.

So the safest pour is the worthless one, and greed and survival are the same act тАФ the closer to the
rim you dare land, the more it pays and the less room the carry has. That is the core-fun question
answered *inside the mechanic*, which is what the depth standard asks for.

**Varied structure + progression from birth** (10th game on the pattern). A run is a seeded sequence
of named pours from a stage-weighted pool: **Steady** (calm on-ramp) ┬╖ **Slow Draw** (the flow drops
to a trickle and the line sits high тАФ the easiest vessels in the game *on purpose*: the **greed
window**, where a cool head chains brims) ┬╖ **Stutter** (trickle/gush alternating тАФ the carry changes
size every vessel, so a memorised release point is worse than useless) ┬╖ **Narrow Neck** (a fast
spout into a high line; the gold flashes past in a handful of ticks) ┬╖ **Hairline** (the line drawn
right up under the gold тАФ reach it at all and you're already brimming) ┬╖ **The Flood** (the late
crescendo). `minStage`-gated, so climbing the stages *opens the pool*: calm share >75% тЖТ <40% from
Drip to Whitewater (pinned by a test).

**Honest difficulty, made structural.** A formation's `flow` is only a **multiplier on the score's
own ramp** тАФ `flowRate()` = `flowScale()` ├Ч the vessel's flow, band-clamped `[0.65, 1.65]` and
hard-capped тАФ so no pour can spike past the pressure the run has earned. `flowScale()` is a smooth
asymptote (├Ч1 тЖТ ├Ч1.55), never a plateau. (Same guardrail Skyline's wind got yesterday; built in from
day one here.)

**Depth inside the verb.** The hidden **meniscus** (a razor sub-window at the very top of the gold тАФ
never taught, never drawn), **Surge** (a meniscus streak тЖТ a ~7 s double-score window), and a
**secret 6th stage** (Whitewater). The shell *teaches* the gold band, and draws the droplets still
in the air тАФ the carry is shown as the physical fact it is тАФ but it never draws where they will
land. That gap is the skill.

**Tests.** 36 pure-core tests: the delay line (nothing lands for LAG ticks; a vessel settles at
exactly `level + carry`), all three commit branches, the brim/meniscus windows and the multiplier
cap, Surge being exactly double, the honest-difficulty hard cap, `setVessel`'s clamps (incl. the
invariant that `LINE_MAX < 1 тИТ BRIM_BAND`, so a "landed but timid" band always exists), the
varied-structure suite (pool well-formed ┬╖ stage-eligible + deterministic pick ┬╖ calm share collapses
┬╖ distinct seeds тЖТ distinct structures ┬╖ the queue never empties), the meta reducer, determinism, and
the frame-one guard. The nicest one: a **carry-blind bot** тАФ a player who watches the level and
ignores the stream in the air тАФ is asserted to *always eventually spill*. That test *is* the design.
Collection **482/482** green.

**Preview.** Chrome MCP was unavailable again, so validated with a real **headless-Chrome render** of
the live game (temp probe: the real shell, driven by synthetic key events, with a carry-aware bot).
Worth it тАФ **it caught a genuine defect**: the multiplier readout sat at `top:70px`, dead centre,

## GROW тАФ Skyline onto varied structure (the wind). v0.21.1.

**The gap.** Skyline was one of three games still off the varied-structure pattern (with Loft and
Poise). Its slab came from a single flat rule in `spawnCurrent`: a random edge-safe start, a random
heading, the score's slide speed. The *only* thing that ever changed across a run тАФ or between runs
тАФ was how fast the slab slid. Every tower rose the same way.

**The change.** A run is now a seeded **sequence of named wind patterns**. Copied Polarity's shape
into Skyline's own core (`FORMATIONS` / `pickFormation` / `loadFormation`, self-contained тАФ no
shared runtime); `spawnCurrent` pulls one `{fx, dir, speedMul}` spec at a time and refills from a
fresh, stage-eligible formation when the queue is spent.

| Pattern | minStage | Notable | What it does |
|---|---|---|---|
| Steady | 0 | тАФ | the calm on-ramp (the old generator, kept) |
| Crosswind | 0 | тАФ | slabs enter hard against alternating edges; long, readable sweeps |
| Plumb Line | 1 | тЬФ | wind drops тАФ slow (0.75├Ч), near-centre slabs: the **flush-streak window** |
| Gust | 1 | тЬФ | a short run of fast slabs (1.22тАУ1.40├Ч) thrown in from an edge |
| Shear | 2 | тЬФ | crawling тЖФ racing slabs (0.8├Ч / 1.42├Ч) тАФ rhythm is useless, read each one |
| The Squall | 3 | тЬФ | Spire-only crescendo: near-max-speed slabs (1.45тАУ1.55├Ч), alternating edges |

`minStage` gates each тЖТ climbing the stages **opens the pool** (progression drives the variation);
`weight(stage)` leans on the wild wind late. Calm share falls **>75% (Foundation) тЖТ <40% (Spire)**,
pinned by a test.

**The design call worth recording: the wind is a *multiplier on the honest ramp*, not a new axis.**
`slabSpeed()` = `speedOf()` ├Ч the formation's `speedMul`, clamped to `[SPEED_MUL_MIN=0.7,
SPEED_MUL_MAX=1.55]` and hard-capped at `SPEED_HARD_MAX=12`. So a formation can *shape* the
difficulty the score already earned, but can never spike past it тАФ the varied-structure standard's
"honest difficulty" guardrail, made structural rather than a promise. A slab with no `speedMul`
(e.g. one a test builds by hand) rides the plain ramp, so the legacy shape still works.

**Plumb Line is the interesting one.** It's the *only* formation that makes the game easier тАФ and
that's the point: it's the greed beat. Slow, centred slabs are the best flush-streak window in the
game, and Skyline's escalating streak bonus (2nd flush +1, 3rd +2 тАж) means noticing it and
committing is worth real points. It's notable, so it announces itself. That's a decision the player
gets to make, which is more interesting than a difficulty spike.

**Legibility.** The cue (`тЧЗ PLUMB LINE`) names notable patterns only; calm ones pass silently. And
the wind reads on the canvas *before* it's named: a slab with `speedMul > 1.1` drags a short motion
streak and burns brighter (`prefers-reduced-motion` honoured, streak suppressed). Start-panel copy
names the pattern set so a new player knows runs vary.
